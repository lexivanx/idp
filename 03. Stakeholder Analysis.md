## 3. Stakeholder Analysis

### 3.1 Primary Personas

**1: Application Developer**
- Role: Backend/Frontend developer on a product team
- Pain Points: Spends 20%+ of time on infrastructure tasks; doesn't understand Kubernetes; blocked waiting for DevOps team to provision resources
- Needs: One-click service creation, environment management, log access, deployment triggers
- Success: Ships features without touching infrastructure YAML

**2: Tech Lead**
- Role: Technical lead responsible for a product vertical
- Pain Points: No visibility into service health across their domain; inconsistent practices across team members; struggles to enforce standards
- Needs: Service catalog overview, dependency mapping, compliance dashboards, golden path enforcement
- Success: All services in their domain follow organizational standards without manual review

**3: Platform Engineer**
- Role: Member of the platform team maintaining CloudMultiPloy
- Pain Points: Constant interrupt-driven work from product teams; every team wants something slightly different; no standardization
- Needs: Extensible template system, plugin architecture, clear API contracts, self-service that actually works
- Success: Spends 80%+ time on platform improvements, not ad-hoc requests

**4: Engineering Manager**
- Role: Engineering manager overseeing multiple teams
- Pain Points: No unified view of operational health; cost attribution is manual; incident response is chaotic
- Needs: Cost dashboards, SLO tracking, incident timeline, team velocity metrics
- Success: Data-driven decisions about infrastructure investment and team capacity

### 3.2 Secondary Stakeholders

- **Security/Compliance Team**: Needs audit trails, policy enforcement, secret management integration
- **Finance Team**: Needs cloud cost attribution per team/service/environment
- **SRE/On-Call Engineers**: Need runbooks, incident management integration, and clear escalation paths
